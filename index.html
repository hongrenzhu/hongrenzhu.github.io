Selected Research Experience

(Selected Thesis) Research on Neural Network Loss Functions and Optimization Methods — A Case Study on the MNIST Dataset. Compared three common loss functions and three mainstream optimizers for handwritten digit recognition using PyTorch-built CNNs, analysing accuracy, F1-score, convergence speed, and training stability; the combination of Focal Loss and SGD achieved the best performance (test accuracy ≈ 99.16%, F1-score ≈ 0.9916).

(Honours Thesis) A Comparative Study of Various Neural Network Architectures on CIFAR-10. Evaluated SNN, FNN, DNN, CNN, and ResNet in terms of training dynamics, final performance, and parameter efficiency; CNN was the most efficient in terms of parameters, while ResNet achieved the highest accuracy (~93.68%) at the cost of efficiency.

Data Analysis in Musquash Marine Protected Area. Analysed biodiversity and sediment characteristics using 2010–2023 monitoring data, applying linear regression, GAMs, and regression trees; identified significant seasonal and spatial variations in biodiversity with sediment texture and organic content as key explanatory variables.
